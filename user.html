TERMS AND CONDITIONS

By submitting this Learning and Development Nomination Form, the parties agree to the following conditions regarding attendance and substitution:

  1. ADVANCE NOTICE OF CANCELLATION. The Primary Participant shall notify the appropriate office at least three (3) days prior to the training if he/she will not pursue the scheduled Learning & Development Intervention (LDI).
  2. FORMAL JUSTIFICATION AND SUBSTITUTION. The Primary Participant must provide a formal written justification for declining the training. Upon this formal notification, the Alternate/Substitute Participant (ASP) will be endorsed to attend the training.
  3. SAME-DAY CANCELLATION. In cases in which the Primary Participant declines the training on the actual day of the event, the cancellation shall only be accepted if it is due to a major reason.
  4. ACCEPTABLE MAJOR REASONS. Valid major reasons for same-day cancellation and the immediate endorsement of the Alternate/Substitute Participant (ASP) include the following:
    1. Illness.
    2. Force majeure.
    3. Death.
    4. Urgent and critical official business directives from the department.
  5. RELEVANCE TO FUNCTIONS. The nomination to the LDI must be formally justified based on its relevance to the participants actual functions and specific development needs. The intervention must purposefully address identified core, leadership, or functional competencies.
  6. APPROVAL AND ENDORSEMENT. The nomination, including the designation of the ASP, is only considered valid and final upon the complete briefing of the nominee and the official signature of the Office/Unit Head.

    01 CITIZEN'S CHARTER (CC)

    The crafting of the Citizen's Charter, in accordance with Republic Act 11032 and its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), marks a significant milestone in the pursuit of good governance and public service excellence. RA 11032, also known as the Ease of Doing Business and Efficient Government Service Delivery Act of 2018, aims to streamline bureaucratic processes, reduce red tape, and promote transparency and efficiency in the delivery of government services. The Citizen's Charter serves as a vital tool in achieving these objectives, as it provides a comprehensive guide that outlines the rights and responsibilities of citizens and establishes clear standards for public service delivery. The meticulous development of the Citizen's Charter, guided by the principles of accountability and public participation, ensures that the government's commitment to providing prompt, efficient, and quality services to its constituents is upheld.

      Cash Division

      Competency Report

      Introduction

      The Cash Division plays a pivotal role in the financial operations of the Department of Transportation (DOTr). Tasked with managing cash flows, processing disbursements, and ensuring that financial resources are allocated accurately and timely, the division is the backbone of the department's fiscal execution.

      "The objective of this Competency Assessment is to establish a clear baseline of the required skills to maintain excellence in financial accountability and service delivery."

      This report documents the findings from the recent Competency Validation exercise, aimed at identifying skill gaps and formulating targeted Learning and Development (L&D) interventions for the personnel of the Cash Division.

      Data Gathering

      100%

      Participation Rate

      All 19 personnel from the Cash Division successfully participated in the assessment.

      3

      Methodologies Used

      Digital Self-Assessments, One-on-one Supervisor Interviews, and Desk Reviews.

      Data was systematically extracted from the HRDD Learning Portal database. Self-assessments were cross-referenced with immediate supervisor evaluations to ensure a balanced and objective data pool regarding the current skills landscape.

      Procedure

      Phase 1: Orientation

      Briefing of Cash Division personnel on the importance of the Competency Assessment and navigation of the digital portal.

      Phase 2: Execution & Submission

      Employees provided detailed input regarding their routine tasks, secondary duties, and perceived proficiency in required competencies.

      Phase 3: Supervisor Validation

      Immediate supervisors reviewed the self-reported skill levels, amended evaluations where necessary, and provided final clearance.

      Phase 4: Consolidation & Analysis

      HRDD synthesized the data to map out the baseline competency levels and identify existing gaps across the division.

      Demographics Overview

      19 Total Personnel

      Contract of Service / COS (5) 26%
      Job Order / JO (3) 16%

      Data indicates a strong base of permanent employees handling core financial operations, supported by COS and JO personnel managing specialized or administrative functions.

      Based on the identified competency gaps, the following Learning and Development (L&D) Interventions are strongly recommended for the Cash Division personnel:
